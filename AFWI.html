AFWI Rules & Mechanics Reference Guide

1. Action Economy, Turn Structure & Passing
During your active Turn, you are allocated a global shared action pool consisting of exactly 1 Move, 1 Acquire, and 1 Shoot action. You do not have to use all actions, and they can be executed by different tokens in any sequence. Additionally, you are limited to activating exactly 1 Squadron card per turn. A round continues with alternating turns until both commanders choose to consecutively PASS, ending the current ATO Cycle.
2. The ATO Cycle Phases
Every campaign round runs through a rigid multi-domain sequence:
  • Setup & Deployment: Select Postures, select covert Missions, and draft squadrons/enablers.
  • Initiative Bid: Sacrifice Enabler cards secretly to modify a 1d4 roll. High roll wins Initiative.
  • Play Intel: Roll 1d4 (with Advantage for initiative winner) to sweep/unmask enemy Enabler cards. At least 1 card remains hidden.
  • MAS Action Phase: Alternating commander turns utilizing the Move-Acquire-Shoot combat engine.
  • End of Round Cleanup: The tactical board clears for the next ATO. Squadron HP and aircraft losses persist in the campaign ledger. A redrafted squadron returns at its normal starting location with only its surviving aircraft unless an Enabler changes that state. Any airborne assets whose parent Squadron Cards were destroyed crash during land-back attrition.
3. Bidding for Initiative & the Cyber Track
The commander who wins the Initiative Bid gains immediate Intel Advantage. They also get a free chance to advance their Cyber Rate on their private Cyber Track (0 to 4). To advance, you must roll equal to or higher than the target rate (e.g., to go from 1 to 2, roll a 2+ on 1d4). Instant Victory: Attaining a Cyber Rate of 4 immediately ends the game with a win. Cyber Advantage (holding a higher Cyber Rate than your opponent) grants Advantage (+1 d4 roll) on all Acquisition checks.
4. Intel Domain, Active ID & Fog of War
Opponents' squadron and enabler hands are MASKED. Assets spawned on the map begin as unacquired UNIDENTIFIED TRACKS with locked details. You must paint them via Acquisition before they can be shot. If you hold Intel Advantage at round start, you roll the d4 with Advantage. Intel Rating: Intel is a persistent, non-spendable rating used by scenario effects and information-advantage mechanics. AWACS and bases automatically reveal certain targets on sensory contact.
5. Static Board Geography & Movement
The theater map consists of a static layout with 5 consolidated central tactical movement bands (Lanes 1 to 5), flanked by each side's respective Airbase and Standoff zones. The PRC commander plays top-down (bottom bands are closer to US, top bands closer to PRC), while the US commander plays bottom-up. Standard aircraft have a Move Range of 2 bands, while heavy bombers and AEW platforms have a Move Range of 1 band. Static assets (SAMs) cannot move.
6. Combat, Munitions, & the Winchester Rule
Fighters, bombers, and ships must roll equal to or higher than the target's acquisition threshold (usually 2+) to score a hit. Upon firing a shot, you must roll a d4 to check weapon depletion:
  • Winchester Penalty: If the roll is a 1, 2, or 3, the unit expends its ordnance and is marked as Winchester [W]. It must return to its home airbase to re-arm at the beginning of its next turn.
  • Ammo Conservation: Rolling a Natural 4 represents a critical success where the asset destroys its target without exhausting its payload. It remains armed on the board.
  • Infinite Drone Ammo: Drones (UAS) carry highly efficient payloads and only go Winchester on a roll of 1 or 2.
  • Exploding Dice: Heavy ground strikes (bombers/missiles) rolling a Natural 4 trigger "Exploding Dice", allowing an immediate bonus 1d4 damage roll.
7. Symmetrical Missile Defense (WEZ Intercepts)
Ground-based ADA (SAMs) and Guided Missile Destroyers (SAG) project a Weapon Engagement Zone (WEZ) equal to their shoot range. Any offensive strike path originating in or transiting through an enemy asset's WEZ triggers an active intercept warning. The defending player can activate Missile Defense, forcing the attacker's hit and damage rolls to suffer Disadvantage (roll 2 d4 and take the lower value). Naval units must cover a physical Salvo Block to perform this, whereas static SAM units have infinite defensive payloads.
8. Deployed Squadron Damage & Attrition
Unlike standard tokens which are destroyed in 1 hit, Deployed Squadrons act as command cards with 2 HP and are placed directly on their side's airbase. Their health and surviving aircraft count are displayed on the card. Deployed airborne tokens are linked back to their parent squadron. If a squadron card is reduced to 0 HP, its base structure is ruined. Any associated airborne tokens on the board are marked as Rogue or must land at round end. Attempting to land back on a destroyed squadron card causes immediate crash-destruction.

Airbase Strikes: Airbases are always identified and require no Acquisition. Each airbase may be successfully struck up to three times during the campaign. Every successful strike awards the attacker 1 VP. Airbase strikes cause no other effect, and an airbase cannot be destroyed.

Military Asset Glossary & Strategic Role Playbook

5th Generation Stealth Fighters (F-22 Raptor, F-35A Lightning, J-20 Dragon)
Strategic Role: The premier air-dominance platforms. Equipped with advanced stealth cross-sections, they are extremely difficult for enemy radars to paint, granting them a baseline `+1` Acquisition Bonus (or hiding their properties entirely until scanned).
  • Combat Profile: High speed (Move Range: 2), moderate sensor suite, and high weapon lethality at short range (Shoot Range: 1).
  • Employment: Hunt enemy stealth fighters, run vanguard sweeps to clear paths for bombers, and intercept incoming strike groups. Multi-role F-35s can carry ground munitions.
4th Generation Tactical Fighters (F-15EX Strike Eagle, J-16 Flanker, J-10C Firebird)
Strategic Role: As heavy loadout workhorses, 4th Gen fighters lack the stealth of 5th Gen platforms but compensate with massive, versatile payloads capable of firing both Air-to-Air (AAM) and Air-to-Ground (AGM) missiles.
  • Combat Profile: High speed (Move Range: 2), standard sensor suite, and devastating multi-role strikes at close-combat range (Shoot Range: 1).
  • Employment: Exploit holes in enemy air defenses created by stealth vanguards, escort high-value AWACS platforms, and execute kinetic ground-strike packages on airfields.
Standoff Heavy Bombers (B-52H Stratofortress, H-6K Badger)
Strategic Role: Heavy long-range strategic bombers. They operate exclusively from the safety of home Standoff zones, completely out of reach of ordinary fighter aircraft.
  • Combat Profile: Very slow (Move Range: 1), poor sensors, but equipped with devastating standoff cruise missiles capable of striking targets across the entire map (Shoot Range: 6/5).
  • Employment: Target and systematically dismantle static enemy airbase structures and ground radars from extreme distances. Bomber strikes inherently trigger Exploding d4 damage, but they depend on AEW AWACS to paint target locks first.
Unmanned Aerial Systems / UAS Combat Drones (MQ-9 Reaper, TB-001 Twin-Tailed Scorpion)
Strategic Role: Slower, expendable, cost-effective remotely piloted vehicles used to sustain continuous ground-strike pressure without risking pilot lives.
  • Combat Profile: Slow speed (Move Range: 1), standard sensors, and close range air-to-ground missiles (Shoot Range: 1).
  • Employment: UAS drones feature unique "Infinite Ammo" payloads, meaning they only go Winchester on a low roll of 1 or 2. Use them as persistent harassment assets to force SAM units to waste their ammo or slowly chip away at naval hulls.
Airborne Early Warning / AEW AWACS (E-3 Sentry, KJ-500)
Strategic Role: High-altitude flying radar and battle management systems. They carry zero weapons but represent the eyes and ears of the theater commander.
  • Combat Profile: Slow speed (Move Range: 1), zero weapons, but project an extreme sensor suite (Sensor Range: 3) with a massive `+2` Acquisition Roll Bonus.
  • Employment: Strategically park AWACS in Standoff zones. Due to their sensor suite, they can attempt two acquisitions per turn, sweeping away enemy fog of war to paint targets for fighters and heavy bombers. Protect them at all costs.
Surface-to-Air Missile Batteries / SAM ADA (Patriot, S-400 Triumf)
Strategic Role: Strategic ground-based air defenses placed on airfields to deny the enemy unrestricted airspace access.
  • Combat Profile: Stationary (Move Range: 0), strong radar sensors, and long-range interceptors (Shoot Range: 3/4).
  • Employment: SAM batteries establish lethal Weapon Engagement Zones (WEZs). Any enemy attack originating from or transiting through their sector automatically triggers active missile defense, forcing incoming enemy rolls to suffer Disadvantage. They hold infinite defensive reloads.
Naval guided Missile Destroyers / SAG Flotillas (DDG-81, Type 055 Renhai)
Strategic Role: Mobile, heavily armored surface combatants that act as floating multi-mission fortresses.
  • Combat Profile: Moderate speed (Move Range: 1), deep magazines represented by finite Salvo Blocks, and heavy air defense (Shoot Range: 3).
  • Employment: Sail guided-missile destroyers into forward lanes to extend a mobile protective air defense umbrella (WEZ) over advancing fighters. They must expend their air salvo blocks to perform active missile defense intercepts, but they can also launch offensive kinetic strikes against bases.
